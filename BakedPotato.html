Microwave Baked Potato

Engineering Steam & Texture

Prep Time: 2 mins

Cook Time: 7-10 mins

Hardness: Beginner (focus on timing)

The Components

  • 1 Large Russet Potato (starchy variety)
  • 1 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • Flaky Sea Salt & Cracked Black Pepper
  • Optional: Sour cream, chives, or butter

The Transformation

  1. The Ventilation: Scrub the potato clean. Use a fork to pierce the skin 6–8 times deeply. This prevents a "steam explosion."
  2. The Coating: Rub the skin with olive oil and a generous amount of salt. This aids in heat conduction.
  3. The Microwave: Cook on high for 5 minutes. Flip the potato (carefully!) and cook for another 3–5 minutes until soft.
  4. The Resting: Let it sit for 2 minutes. The residual heat finishes the center.

Technical Notes

"The fork piercings are the most critical 'engineering' step. Without them, internal pressure builds until the skin fails."

The Final Plate (Plating)

Slice a cross into the top and squeeze the ends to bloom the white, fluffy interior. Top with a perfect cube of butter right in the center for a minimalist, geometric presentation.
