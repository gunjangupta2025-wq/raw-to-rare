Raspberry Yoghurt Ice Cream

The Science of Controlled Freezing

Prep Time: 10 mins

Freeze Time: 4-6 hours

Hardness: Intermediate (crystal control)

The Components

  • 2 cups Fresh Raspberries (macerated)
  • 2 cups Full-Fat Greek Yoghurt
  • 1/4 cup Honey or Maple Syrup
  • 1 tsp Lemon Juice (to stabilize color)
  • A pinch of Sea Salt

The Transformation

  1. The Maceration: Crush the raw raspberries with lemon juice and honey. Let sit for 10 minutes to extract the natural syrups.
  2. The Fold: Gently fold the berry reduction into the yoghurt. Avoid over-mixing if you want a "rare" marbled visual effect.
  3. The Initial Chill: Pour into a shallow container. The increased surface area ensures a faster, more uniform freeze.
  4. The Agitation: Whisk the mixture every 45 minutes for the first 3 hours. This breaks up ice crystals, ensuring a professional, creamy texture.

Technical Notes

"The agitation process is the 'engineering' behind the silkiness. Without regular breaking of the crystals, the water content in the yoghurt would create a jagged, icy structure."

The Final Plate (Plating)

Serve in a chilled glass bowl to slow the melting process. Use a heated scoop to create a perfect sphere, and garnish with three whole raw raspberries placed in a triangular formation.
