Satay Tofu Power Bowl

The Engineering of Plant-Based Texture

Prep Time: 15 mins

Cook Time: 10 mins

Hardness: Intermediate (moisture management)

The Components

  • 1 block Extra Firm Tofu (pressed)
  • 2 cups Shredded Red Cabbage & Carrots
  • 1/4 cup Natural Peanut Butter
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce & 1 tsp Sriracha
  • Fresh Cilantro & Lime Wedges

The Transformation

  1. The Extraction: Press the raw tofu for 10 minutes to remove excess water. This is the most critical step for a successful sear.
  2. The Sear: Cube the tofu and cook in a hot pan until all sides achieve a golden-brown, rigid crust.
  3. The Emulsion: Whisk peanut butter, soy sauce, and sriracha with a splash of warm water until the fats emulsify into a smooth sauce.
  4. The Assembly: Layer the warm, seared tofu over the raw, crunchy vegetables to create a contrast in both temperature and texture.

Technical Notes

"The Maillard reaction on the tofu provides the 'Rare' savory depth that anchors the dish against the high-acid, raw vegetable base."

The Final Plate (Plating)

Arrange components in distinct quadrants within a deep bowl. Drizzle the satay sauce in a precise grid pattern and finish with a cluster of cilantro in the center for an organized, professional aesthetic.
