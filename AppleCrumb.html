Healthy Apple Crumble

The Science of Caramelized Texture

Prep Time: 15 mins

Bake Time: 30 mins

Hardness: Intermediate (moisture balance)

The Components

  • 4 Large Granny Smith Apples (peeled and sliced)
  • 1 cup Rolled Oats (for the structural crunch)
  • 1/2 cup Almond Flour
  • 2 tbsp Maple Syrup
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon & a pinch of Sea Salt

The Transformation

  1. The Base: Toss the raw apple slices with cinnamon and a splash of water. This creates the steam necessary for the "soft" phase.
  2. The Crumble: Mix oats, almond flour, and maple syrup until "pebbly." This texture is the key to an engineered crunch.
  3. The Layering: Spread the apples evenly in a dish and top with the crumble. Do not pack it down; airflow is required for crisping.
  4. The Heat: Bake at 190°C. The goal is bubbling fruit beneath a golden-brown, rigid surface.

Technical Notes

"Using Granny Smith apples is a strategic choice; their high acidity prevents them from turning into mush, keeping the 'Rare' structural integrity of the slice."

The Final Plate (Plating)

Serve warm in a shallow ceramic bowl. Contrast the rustic crumble with a precise, cold dollop of Greek yogurt or a drizzle of honey to bridge the gap between temperatures.
